Pokemon 151 Challenge - Gen 3 Discussion

Published April 20, 2025

What is the Pokemon 151 Challenge?

Earlier in the year, I put together some rules for a narrative-driven challenge of Pokemon Gold… the 151 Challenge! I wanted to breathe new life into the game without making it too difficult. The Nuzlocke challenge is popular, but I’m not really interested in that. I prefer to keep my Pokemon, build a consistent team and have more freedom when catching new Pokemon in the wild. There are also Type-only playthroughs on YouTube, which I would be more interested in trying out at some point. Instead, I decided to challenge myself to play through the game by battling with only Gen 1 Pokemon.

There are some basic rules, which you can find on my playthrough videos on YouTube. For example, early in the game you must catch a proxy Gen 1 starter Pokemon to replace your Gen 2 starter. The real challenge is the Elite Four because you are limited in terms of being able to fully evolve your Gen 1 Pokemon (evolution stones aren’t available and trade-only evolutions won't work if you're using an emulator). Apart from that, the rest of the game is straightforward. The challenge makes you think about the map in a different way. You learn more about each Route and other areas that would normally be overlooked, which is great. It's really designed to tap into that 90's nostalgia for the original 151 generation.

Okay, but will it work in Gen 3?

Yes and no. Can you play through every gym and become the champion in Pokemon Ruby/Sapphire with only Gen 1 Pokemon? It doesn’t seem possible. In Pokemon Emerald, Abra can be caught in the wild before taking on the first gym leader in Rustboro City. Unfortunately, Abra won’t be able to deal any damage until it evolves into Kadabra at Level 16. This means that you would need to grind with your Gen 3 starter a lot before you could use your proxy. Technically, I guess it is possible in Emerald. However, for the challenge to work as intended, the player’s Gen 3 starter would need to be swapped out as soon as their Gen 1 proxy is caught, as per the original rules.

As I’ll be playing Pokemon Ruby, here’s how it could work… You will need to defeat Roxanne with your Gen 3 starter. There’s no way around it. To gain access to Dewford Town, you will need to do that. Once you do reach Dewford Town, you can catch a variety of Gen 1 Pokemon. In Granite Cave, you can find Zubat, Abra or Geodude in the wild. By fishing, you may also encounter Tentacool, Magikarp or Goldeen (if you backtrack a little). It’s important to remember to catch a Pokemon that you can battle with straight away. You will need to store your Gen 3 starter in the PC once you catch your proxy. From there, the Heonn region is yours to explore! I will draft rules more formally soon. I’m still trying to defeat the Elite Four in Pokemon Gold with my team of Gen 1 Pokemon.. Be sure to check that out in the meantime.
